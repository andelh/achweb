I've been heads down building lately. Not _only_ for clients, but for something more personal, tools that solve real problems for myself and the people I know.

Four SaaS apps: **Taack**, **Dollah**, **Gazette**, and **The WiFy Plugin**. All built with the same stack: [Next.js](https://nextjs.org), [Convex](https://convex.dev), and [Clerk](https://clerk.com). Honestly? It's all I need these days.

The goal isn't just passive income (though that's a nice plus). It's about making a tangible difference in the lives of people in Trinidad & Tobago and beyond. Here's what's been cooking:

## Dollah

**Goal: 100 monthly users | Current: 6/100**

[Dollah](https://dollah.app) is an automatic spending tracker that's actually made for the Caribbean. Without Open Banking in the region, developers have no way of accessing transaction data securely from banks. Instead, Dollah reads your transaction alert emails from Republic Bank and automatically parses and categorizes everything. Groceries, gas, food—it's all there, color-coded and ready to go.

The app is in early access right now, and I'm focused on perfecting the Republic Bank experience before rolling out support for other banks. It's been interesting seeing how people react when they realize their spending habits are way different than they thought.

## Taack

**Goal: $1,000,000 in revenue in the next year**

Yeah, I said it. [Taack](https://taack.app) is the event ticketing platform Trinidad and Tobago deserves. Clean event pages, local payments (finally!), fast payouts, and analytics that actually help you understand your audience.

The platform handles everything from yoga retreats to full-scale productions. Fees are transparent—7% on paid tickets, $1.80 TTD flat per order, and free events stay free. Promoters get paid within 3 days after their event wraps. No surprises, no delays.

I'm betting big on this one. If we can become the go-to platform for local events, that million-dollar goal isn't as crazy as it sounds.

## The WiFy Plugin

**Goal: 500 subscribers**

[The WiFy Plugin](https://thewifyplugin.com) bridges a massive gap in the Caribbean e-commerce world. It's a Shopify app that integrates WiPay so store owners can accept local payments in JMD, TTD, BBD, and USD—without the usual headaches.

Setup takes 2 days, funds go straight to your WiPay account, and there are no hidden service charges. We're already at 306+ happy customers, and the feedback has been solid. The Caribbean Shopify community is growing, and I want this plugin to be the obvious choice for anyone serious about selling online.

## Gazette

**Goal: 1,500 daily active users**

[Gazette](https://ttgazette.com) is my news aggregator for Trinidad and Tobago. All the headlines, one clean feed. No jumping between tabs, no missing stories. Just everything happening in T&T, updated in real time.

I'm still building out features, but the vision is simple: become the homepage for Trinis who want to stay informed. If I can hit 1,500 daily actives, I'll know we've created something people actually rely on.

## Why This Stack?

Next.js for the frontend and routing, Convex for real-time backend magic, and Clerk for auth that just works. It's fast, it's reliable, and I can spin up new features without overthinking infrastructure. When you're building four apps at once, simplicity matters.
