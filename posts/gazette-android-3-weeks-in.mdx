## The Surprise

I'm **surprised but happy** with the number of people continuing on to read the full story. **73%** is way higher than I expected for a news aggregator.

It tells me that:
1. People are finding articles they actually care about
2. The headlines are compelling enough to click through
3. Gazette is solving a real discovery problem, not just being a "news scanner"

That 73% rate is honestly one of the most validating metrics I could ask for.

## What I'm Learning

The **weekly active user** number could definitely be improved. I'm learning that **a lot of people are quite content with just the notifications** and open the app less often.

Which is... actually fine?

Some users want the full feed experience. Others just want breaking news alerts. Both are valid use cases. I need to optimize for both, not just assume everyone wants to scroll through dozens of articles daily.

## User Behavior Insights

Here's what the data is showing me about how Trinis consume news:

- **Morning spike**: Most opens happen between 6-9am
- **Lunch check-in**: Another bump around 12-2pm
- **Evening catch-up**: Light activity between 6-8pm
- **Notification-driven**: Major stories drive instant traffic

The **44% returning user rate** after one week is encouraging. Almost half the people who install stick around beyond the first week. That's a good foundation to build on.

## The Tech Behind It

For the curious, Gazette's Android app is built with:

- **React Native** — Cross-platform from day one (iOS coming soon)
- **Convex** — Real-time backend, scheduled news fetching, user data sync
- **Expo** — Simplified build process and OTA updates
- **Clerk** — Authentication across web and mobile
- **TypeScript** — Type safety across the entire stack

The real MVP here is Convex. Their real-time sync means when news breaks, everyone gets it instantly. No manual refreshing. No stale feeds. Just works.

## What's Next?

I'm going to **iterate on what's working**:

1. **Better notifications** — Smarter alerting for breaking news vs. routine updates
2. **Personalization** — Let users follow specific sources or topics they care about
3. **Improved onboarding** — Help new users understand the notification/feed balance
4. **Share features** — Make it easy to share articles with friends/family
5. **Performance tuning** — Faster load times, smoother scrolling

The goal is to hit **1,500 daily active users** by year end. We're not there yet, but three weeks in, I'm feeling optimistic.
