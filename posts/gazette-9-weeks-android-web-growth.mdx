### The 23% Mystery

Here's the fascinating part: **only 23% of web users** click through to read the full story, compared to **71% on Android**.

Why?

I have a theory:

- **Desktop users are scanners** — They want the headlines at a glance, then move on
- **Mobile users are divers** — When they open an app, they're committed to reading
- **Context matters** — Web traffic comes from social (Facebook, Twitter), so people are less engaged
- **Intent is different** — App users deliberately downloaded Gazette; web users might just stumble in

This is teaching me that **platform behavior is wildly different**. I need to optimize each experience for its actual usage pattern, not assume they're the same.

## The Growth Strategy

I mentioned in my last post I'd iterate on what works. Here's what I've been doing:

### Google Ads Experiment

I'm running a **small Google ads campaign**:
- **$1.50/day** budget
- **~$0.03 cost per click** (CPC)
- Targeting Trinidad & Tobago + diaspora in major cities
- Focused on "Trinidad news" and related keywords

It's not a massive spend, but it's enough to test what works. I'll **definitely iterate** on the successful ads and **scale this up** once I find the right formula.

### Facebook Traffic

A lot of web traffic has been coming from **Facebook**. So I've been trying to encourage **article sharing** within the app and on web.

The hypothesis: If users can quickly share interesting articles to their timeline or WhatsApp, Gazette becomes a tool for staying informed AND keeping friends informed.

Early results are promising. More on that experiment soon.

## The Technical Evolution

Behind the scenes, I've been leveling up the infrastructure:

- **Faster article fetching** — Using Convex's scheduled functions to check sources every 5 minutes
- **Better caching** — Less loading time, especially on web
- **Push notification tuning** — Smarter alerts for breaking news vs. regular updates
- **Dark mode improvements** — Better contrast and readability at night

All of this is powered by:
- **Next.js** (web)
- **React Native** (Android, and soon iOS)
- **Convex** (backend magic)
- **Clerk** (auth)
- **Vercel** (hosting)
